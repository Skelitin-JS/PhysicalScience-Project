Science Booklet|Venus

Venus is the third planet from the sun.

Venus is 67 million miles from the sun.

The diameter of Venus is 7,520 miles!

Venus is named after the Roman Goddess of love and beauty.

The surface temp on Venus is 847 degrees.

Venus has a thick atmosphere filled with carbon dioxide.

Venus is composed of the following: carbon dioxide, 96%; molecular nitrogen, 3.5%; water, 0.02%; trace quantities of carbon monoxide, molecular oxygen, sulfur dioxide, hydrogen chloride, and other gases

Venus gets from 820 fahrenheit to 900.

Venus has a circular orbit.

Venus spins in a retrograde motion.

Venus is 67.626 million miles from the sun.

Venus is 89.21 million miles away from Earth.

Venus is white. It is very light colored, and kind of boring if I'm honest. Not a good picture to have as your wallpaper.

The number of rings Venus has, a whopping 0.

With the extreme heat of Venus, life on it seems impossible at the moment.

Many different telescopes study Venus, some of which including Goldstone Solar System Radar in California, Arecibo Observatory in Puerto Rico, and Green Bank Observatory in West Virginia.

Despite it not being the closest planet to the sun, Venus is hotter than Mercury.
